Signal model: Amplitude Modulation. The demo gate-modulates a carrier at fnote (note freq) by an on/off envelope at frep (repetition rate = BPM×note‑value/60). This produces three families of spectral peaks:

① Envelope harmonics — the gating envelope is a square wave at frep. Its harmonic series (1st, 2nd, 3rd, …) appear at frep, 2frep, 3frep, … These are the low-frequency “rhythm” lines. In the Rhythm regime (< 20 Hz) they are individually audible as a click-train; above ~60 Hz they fuse into a perceived pitch at frep.

② Waveform harmonics — the chosen waveform (sine / square / saw / triangle) contributes its own series anchored at fnote:
• Sine: fundamental only
• Square: odd (1×, 3×, 5×)
• Saw: all integers (1×, 2×, 3×, …)
• Triangle: odd, falling amplitude
These encode the timbre of each note event and are visible regardless of BPM.

③ AM sidebands — the key to this demo. Amplitude modulation places sidebands on each waveform harmonic at fnm ± n·frep (n = 1, 2, 3, …). At low rep rates the sidebands cluster tightly around fnote; as frep rises the sidebands spread apart.

※ Match condition — when frep = fnote (press Match), every sideband lands exactly on a harmonic of the carrier. All partials align — the timbre snaps into a clean harmonic series and the tone becomes a pure pitched note.

‡ Inharmonic regime — when frep > fnote the sidebands overshoot: the 1st upper sideband fnote + frep exceeds twice the carrier. The spectrum becomes non‑integer, producing a clangorous, bell‑like quality. The Nyquist bound limits frep < sample‑rate / 4.

Spectrum view: H1–H5 cursors mark the waveform harmonic series at n·fnote. Blue USB/LSB cursors mark the AM sidebands at fnote ± n·frep — the sum and difference frequencies created by modulation. The ↻ cursor marks frep when it differs from fnote. The ▲ label marks the strongest measured bin. Frequency scale is 0–10 kHz.

Waveform view: first ~4 cycles of the audio buffer, locked so the display is stable. The sharp vertical edges are articulation gating — the waveform abruptly drops to zero when the on‑slot ends. Switch to Legato 100% to see a continuous un‑gated waveform.
